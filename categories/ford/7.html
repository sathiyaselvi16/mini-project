The Suzuki Equator is a midsize pickup truck that was sold in the United States from 2009 to 2012. It was essentially a rebadged version of the Nissan Frontier, with only minor differences, such as a new front end and a rebadged steering wheel. The Equator offered two main engine options: a 2.5-liter four-cylinder engine producing around 152 horsepower, and a 4.0-liter V6 engine with 261 horsepower, with both engine variants offering either rear-wheel or four-wheel drive options. The Equator was available in several configurations, including a Crew Cab with both short and long bed options, and an Extended Cab. Despite being based on a reliable Nissan design, the Suzuki Equator never gained significant traction in the U.S. market, mainly due to Suzuki's limited dealership network and brand presence.
